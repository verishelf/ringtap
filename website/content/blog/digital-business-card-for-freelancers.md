Freelancers need a single place to send clients and prospects: portfolio, contact info, and links. A digital business card does that with one URL.

## Why freelancers use digital cards

- **One link** — Send `ringtap.me/you` instead of multiple links.
- **Portfolio** — Link to your site, Behance, Dribbble, or case studies.
- **Rates and services** — Add a link to your services page or PDF.
- **Booking** — Link to Calendly, Acuity, or your scheduling tool.
- **Always current** — Update projects and links without reprinting.

## What to include

- Name, photo, and title (e.g. “Freelance Designer”)
- Email and phone
- Portfolio or website
- LinkedIn
- Booking or contact form link
- Optional: testimonials or client logos

## Sharing at events

Use an NFC ring or QR code at meetups and conferences. Tap or scan to share your full portfolio and contact info in seconds.

## Pro features that help

- **Unlimited links** — Add all your projects, social, and tools.
- **Analytics** — See who viewed your profile and which links they clicked.
- **Themes** — Match your brand with accent color and fonts.
